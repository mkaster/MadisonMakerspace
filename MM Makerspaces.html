Madison Makerspaces

What’s a makerspace?

A makerspace is just that: a space for making. Oftentimes makerspaces (or hackerspaces, as they’re sometimes called) are physical locations where community members come together to share resources and collaborate on projects. They can be located in schools or libraries, community centers, or independent locations. Sometimes they’re free and sometimes there’s a fee.

I love makerspaces because I want to try all the artsy things, but that can be pricey! Sometimes makerspaces are free and open to the public, like the Bubbler at Madison Central Library. Other times, makerspaces function like a gym; you pay a monthly membership fee and have access to the facility and making equipment. Makerspaces often have equipment for woodwork, welding, stained glass, glassblowing, sewing, laser cutting, coding, 3D printing, and more. Basically, if you want to make something, you probably can get it done at a makerspace.

If you want to learn more about makerspaces in general, try What is a makerspace? or 7 things you should know about makerspaces.
